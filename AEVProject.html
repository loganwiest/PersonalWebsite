Advanced Energy Vehicle Project

The Advanced Energy Vehicle (AEV) Project was a semester long task that challenged students to create an energy efficienct transportation system. Once students were placed in groups of four, they could chose to do their project on anything they desired. An oral presentation in front of students, faculty, and Smart Columbus employees at the end of the semester was then given to showcase each unique project. To see a PDF of the lab report for my group, click HERE


Opportunity Identifications

The first step of the project was to identify a problem with current transportation issues. By doing outside interviews with students and utilizing mathmatical functions in Excel, our group was able to narrow down what some of the issues with campus transportation is, and how we can address it in our project

AEV Design Concept

Next, our group had to prototype what we wanted our vehicle to look like. By using SolidWorks software, we could all brainstorm ideas of what each individual member thought it should look like in their own time, since we only had one set of materials. After testing propellor configurations, figuring out an ideal weight for the vehicle, and determining an athestically pleasing design, our group agreed on a design.

Vehicle Design / Final Testing

After all the testing and brainstorming, our group was then ready to construct our ideal vehicle based off the SolidWorks model. This vehicle would run all of the graded tests we came up with, such as:

- Ability to stop after going a certain distance and proceed afterwards

- Ability to stop when the light sensor is activated by a stop sign, then prodeed after a certain amount of time

- Controlling power so that it is not too slow on inclines, and not too fast on declines

Main Takeaways

One of the biggest things I learned was how to communicate better in a group. This is important because lots of software projects are done in large groups, so being to organize your work so others can understand and asking questions helps the process run more smoothly. I also learned more technical skills, such as SolidWorks and Arduino coding language. SolidWorks is a popular industry strength program that can be used to visually explain anything, and models can also be downloaded to be 3-D printed in labs.
